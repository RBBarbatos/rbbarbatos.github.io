The Party System

A way for a small group of players to stick together and travel as one. Members agree before the group moves, everyone shares the same countdown, and anyone can call the trip off until the very last moment.

Up to 6 members Everyone must agree 5-second countdown Cancel any time

Overview

A party is a temporary group of players who want to play together. The system has two jobs. First, it manages who is in a party and keeps the group within a safe size. Second, it handles the moment a party wants to travel together to another place — making sure the whole group agrees before anyone is moved.

The guiding idea is simple: a group move is a shared decision. No single member can drag everyone else somewhere they did not agree to go, and no member can be left behind by accident. Travel only happens when there is full agreement, and it can always be stopped.

Core concepts

Party

A group of players travelling together. A party exists as long as it has at least one member; when the last member leaves, it dissolves.

Member

Any player who has joined a party. Every member sees the same group information and takes part in every group decision.

Leader

The first person in the party. If the leader leaves, the role passes to the next-longest-standing member so the party always has one.

Capacity

A party holds a maximum of six members. Once it is full, further join attempts are politely turned away until a spot opens.

Initiator

The member who proposes a group teleport. By making the proposal they are counted as already agreeing to it.

Destination

The place the party is proposing to travel to. It is shown to members for context while they decide.

How a group teleport works

From the first proposal to the final move, a group teleport passes through a clear sequence of stages. At each stage the whole party is kept informed.

  1. 1

    Proposal

    A member asks to teleport the party to a destination. That member is treated as having agreed. Nothing moves yet.

  2. 2

    Consent prompt

    Every other member is shown an Accept or Cancel choice. They can see who proposed the trip and how many people still need to respond.

  3. 3

    Collecting the votes

    Responses are gathered as they arrive. The running tally is shared with the party so everyone can see the progress toward agreement.

  4. 4

    The decision

    If every member accepts, the trip is approved and moves on to the countdown. If anyone cancels, or anyone runs out of time, the trip is called off immediately for the whole party.

  5. 5

    Shared countdown

    A five-second countdown appears on every member’s screen at the same time. A cancel option stays available to everyone throughout.

  6. 6

    Travel — or abort

    If no one cancels before the countdown ends, the whole party travels together. If anyone cancels during the countdown, the trip is aborted and no one moves.

The voting rules

The agreement step follows a few strict rules. They are deliberately cautious: when in doubt, the system chooses not to move anyone.

Any cancel stops it

A single Cancel from any member ends the trip immediately for everyone. One objection is enough.

Silence counts as no

If a member does not respond within the time allowed, their silence is treated as a Cancel. No answer is never taken as a yes.

Everyone must say yes

The trip continues only when every member has clearly chosen Accept. Agreement must be unanimous.

Leaving cancels too

If a member disconnects or leaves the party while a trip is being decided, the group no longer has everyone’s agreement, so the trip is called off.

In short: one yes from everyone moves the party; anything else — a no, a silence, or a departure — stops it.

The countdown

Reaching unanimous agreement does not move anyone straight away. Instead the party enters a short, shared five-second countdown. Its purpose is to give everyone a final, visible chance to change their mind.

Synchronised

The same countdown is shown to every member at the same time.

Always cancellable

A cancel option remains for every member during the whole countdown.

All or nothing

One cancel during the countdown aborts the trip for the entire party.

Only when the countdown finishes with no cancellation does the party travel. Until that instant, stopping is always possible.

If a trip is called off

When a trip does not happen, the party is told why, so the result never feels like a glitch. The common reasons are:

  • A member chose Cancel during the vote.
  • A member did not respond in time.
  • A member left the party mid-decision.
  • A member cancelled during the final countdown.
  • The travel itself could not be completed, so no one was moved.

In every one of these cases the outcome is the same and predictable: nobody is moved, and the party stays exactly as it was.

Who decides

Every important decision is made in one central, trusted place — the game itself — rather than on any individual player’s device. Each player’s screen is only a window onto the shared situation: it shows the prompt and the countdown, and it passes along that player’s choices.

Keeping the roster, the vote count, and the timing in one trusted place means the rules apply equally to everyone and cannot be quietly bypassed. The party roster, the tally of who has agreed, and the countdown are all judged centrally, and the final decision to travel is made there too.

One source of truth

The group’s state is kept in a single trusted place that everyone shares.

Screens only display

A player’s view shows the situation and reports their choice; it never decides the result.

Safe by default

Whenever something is uncertain, the system treats it as a cancel and no one moves.

Glossary

Party
A temporary group of up to six players travelling together.
Member
A player who belongs to a party.
Leader
The longest-standing member, who holds the leader role until they leave.
Initiator
The member who proposes a group teleport and is counted as agreeing to it.
Consent prompt
The Accept-or-Cancel choice shown to the other members when a trip is proposed.
Unanimous
Agreed by every member — the only condition under which a trip proceeds.
Timeout
Running out of time to respond, which the system treats as a cancel.
Countdown
The shared five-second final window before travel, during which anyone can still cancel.
Abort
Calling off a trip so that no member is moved.
